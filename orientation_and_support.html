Orientation and support

What we offer / Orientation and support


Welcome to Shane English School Taiwan

We require teachers to be native English speakers who hold a full university degree as well as a teaching/TEFL certificate. In return we offer the opportunity to live in Taiwan and work within a company that is well-established, well-organised, and consistently supportive of its staff.

We have positions available throughout the year and welcome all teachers, new and experienced, to apply to join our lively team of international teachers.

Start with a conversation with our recruitment team

Speak to our recruitment team to see if Shane English School Taiwan is the right fit for you. The team has years of teaching experience as well as an intimate knowledge of life in Taiwan. Simply complete our online application form and the recruitment team will reach out to you within a few days.
The application process requires completing our online application form, submitting the necessary documents (requested by the recruitment team), a short pre-interview task, and an interview via Skype.

The entire application process can be completed within a week in cases where candidates have their documents prepared and ready.

Receive pre-departure support and airport pick up

If your application is successful the recruitment team will continue to communicate closely with you to ensure you arrive on the right day, with all the necessary documents, and that a representative from the school is waiting to meet you at the airport.
From the airport you’ll be taken to your arrival hotel to get some rest before teacher training week begins. We encourage new teachers to arrive on the Wednesday before training week to allow for some recovery time after your trip, a bit of admin, and some time for exploration.

Take part in a week of specialised teacher training

Teacher training is broken into a number of modules that are designed to be beneficial for both new and experienced teachers alike. New teachers will find value in modules focusing on lesson planning, teaching methodologies and classroom management, while experienced teachers will enjoy seeing the curriculum and hearing from our Directors of Studies who have not only taught the curriculum, but have also played a part in creating it.
Training week also includes the opportunity to observe a number of live classes with some of our current teachers at schools around Taipei.

Work permit and working visa provided

There is no need to worry about paperwork or Taiwanese labour laws as your school will handle the process of getting your work permit and work visa for you once you arrive.
Soon after arriving, a staff member will accompany you to a local hospital for a medical check and begin the process of applying for your work permit. Within a few weeks you will receive your ARC card which allows you to live and work legally in Taiwan for the remainder of your contract.

Finding an apartment and opening a bank account

As finding an apartment is a top priority for new teachers, we aim to find permanent accommodation for new teachers within the first ten days.
Soon after you arrive in Taiwan, a member of staff will discuss your apartment requirements with you (area, rent, etc) and arrange to view a few apartments within the days that follow. They will then accompany you to view the apartments and assist you in communicating with landlords and signing a lease.

You’ll also be taken to a local bank to open a personal account in order to be paid your salary. Salaries are paid directly into your bank account once a month.

Get enrolled in the National Health insurance system

All new teachers will be added to the National Health Insurance (NHI) system once they have received their ARC card. Taiwan has some of the best health insurance in the world, with a trip to the dentist or doctor costing less than a McDonalds meal, medicine included.
However, as it can take up to 2 months to join the NHI after arriving, we encourage new teachers to take out their own private insurance to cover them for their first 2 months in Taiwan.

Enjoy ongoing support from Head Office

Orientation and training week is designed to get new teachers set up and settled in Taiwan, but staff support continues for the duration of your time at Shane. Your designated Director of studies will continue to ensure your professional development and act as your contact point for any issues that may arise, both inside or outside the classroom, while Head Office is always willing to help out teachers in any way they can.
What separates Shane from a number of other schools is our focus on teacher happiness and the number of staff dedicated to ensuring the well-being of our teachers.
